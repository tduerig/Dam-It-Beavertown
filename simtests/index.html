Bug Fix: Removing the Tsunami Injector

Before (Tsunami Bug Active)

- Total Flora: 97 (86 Lilies, 11 Cattails)

Damned River:
- Water Coverage: 59%
- Total Flora: 103 (86 Lilies, 17 Cattails)

After (Natural Physics Restored)

Baseline River:
- Water Coverage: 11%
- Total Flora: 13 (12 Lilies, 1 Cattails)

Damned River:
- Water Coverage: 11%
- Total Flora: 15 (15 Lilies, 0 Cattails)

Status: River constrained to deep gorge. Dams pool water safely yielding higher Lily counts.

Change Description

The Tsunami: Our benchmark script was artificially injecting 40.0 units of water per second instantly at the river source, causing the water to immediately violate the boundaries of the steep Humboldt-style gorge slopes adjacent to our river and flood out sideways, returning an incorrect 60% coverage baseline.

The Fix: Removed the manual injection loop completely. WaterEngine now naturally flows at its standard 0.12 source trickle. Furthermore, Test Dams were reconstructed to scale dynamically to physically block the massive `+5` banks instead of being submerged at `y=2`.

Results: Coverage has dramatically normalized. Because the natural river gorge is incredibly deep, a dammed river doesn't spill over in the 20 minute test window—it just pools deeper horizontally within its natural walls, providing a stable, calm spawning area where Lily numbers reliably boost upwards.

Agentic Discovery Notes & Telemetry Feed

The Final Barrier:

1. The Gap Bypass: We previously cast a massive 70-unit wide dam, but the test world scales down via a Z-slope indefinitely before hitting the visual Humboldt Mountains at world `x=60`. Because of this, water easily bypassed the 70 width dam and traced perfectly parallel rivers entirely around the dam! We expanded the mathematical offline generation to effectively **anchor into the Humboldt sides** (130-width), perfectly simulating a water-tight basin that scales dynamically up to heights of `+15`.

2. Procedural Riverbed Heights: We discovered our `bankHeight` was sampling procedurally generated Perlin-noise ravines. Straight and Twisty dams generated with *less than 0 structural height*. We mathematically bound the dam top explicitly off the direct river center channel base to ensure it maintains perfect seal integrity regardless of Perlin variance.
